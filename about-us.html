The Origin of the Depot Dog

Home Depot began selling hot dogs in their stores as a part of their customer service offerings, often setting up hot dog stands or grills near the entrance or in the parking lot. This practice started in the early 1990s. The idea was to provide a convenient and affordable food option for customers who might be hungry while shopping or after completing their home improvement projects. Hot dogs became a popular snack choice for shoppers at Home Depot, and it's a tradition that continues in many of their stores across the United States.

The history of The Home Depot is a compelling tale of entrepreneurial vision and the transformation of a humble hardware store into a global retail powerhouse. Founded in 1978 by Bernie Marcus, Arthur Blank, Pat Farrah, and Ron Brill, this American home improvement giant has become synonymous with DIY enthusiasts and professional contractors alike. From its modest beginnings in Atlanta, Georgia, The Home Depot has charted an extraordinary journey, fueled by innovation, a commitment to customer service, and an unwavering dedication to empowering individuals to take on their home improvement projects. This introductory glimpse into The Home Depot's remarkable history unveils a story of growth, resilience, and a relentless pursuit of excellence in the realm of home improvement retail.

1978

Founded in Atlanta, Georgia, by Bernie Marcus, Arthur Blank, Pat Farrah, and Ron Brill.

1981

Goes public and starts trading on the NASDAQ stock exchange.

1989

Expands internationally with the opening of the first store in Vancouver, Canada.

2000

Launches its e-commerce website, enabling online shopping.

2007

Frank Blake becomes CEO, succeeding co-founder Arthur Blank.

2015

The Home Depot acquires Interline Brands, a distributor and direct marketer of maintenance, repair, and operations products.

2017

The company's sales exceed $100 billion for the first time.

2021

The Home Depot continues to adapt and expand its online shopping and delivery options to meet changing customer needs.
